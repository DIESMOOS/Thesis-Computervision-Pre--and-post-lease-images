\section{Risk Assessment}
\label{sec:riskassessment}

This project has several potential risks related to data annotation, model performance, and technical dependencies. One potential risk concerns the annotation of the DGW housing images. Since the DGW dataset is not labeled at the start of the project, the manual annotation process may require more time than anticipated. To mitigate this risk, the primary experiments rely on publicly available defect datasets obtained from Roboflow, which already contain labeled examples and allow the experiments to proceed independently of the DGW annotation process.

A second risk is model performance and stability. The YOLOv8 detector may require sufficient labeled training data to achieve reliable results, while the outputs generated by the LLaVA model may vary depending on prompt interpretation. To mitigate this risk, pretrained YOLOv8 weights are used and prompts for LLaVA remain fixed throughout the experiments to ensure consistency and reproducibility across evaluations.

Finally, the experiments depend on computational resources such as GPU availability and software configuration. In the event that local resources are insufficient, experiments can be executed using university computing infrastructure or cloud-based environments. These mitigation strategies ensure that the core research questions can still be addressed even if technical limitations arise during the project.
