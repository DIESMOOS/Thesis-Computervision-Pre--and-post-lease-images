\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Recently computer vision has enabled automated inspection across domains such as remote sensing, building assessment, indoor scene understanding, urban monitoring, and medical imaging. These fields use deep learning ranging from CNNs to newer foundation-model and vision-language methods to detect defects, anomalies, and structural changes. Previous work, like the Cheng\cite{Cheng_Huang_Li_Lyu_Xu_Zhao_Zhao_Xiang_2024} review used classical classifiers, while newer studies explore U-Nets, R-CNN, YOLO architectures, and limited applications of Siamese networks. However Siamese modeling is still concentrated in medical imaging, where longitudinal datasets like MSSEG2\cite{MSSEG2_dataset} Kamraoui et al.\cite{Kamraoui_Mansencal_Manjon_Coupé_2022} provide, standardized image pairs.

This focus on medical data introduces a broader gap. Siamese methods are rarely applied in settings with scarce, heterogeneous, and unaligned images conditions common in house inspections, military surface-change monitoring, cadastral boundary shifts, and other low data change detection tasks. Although MSSEG2\cite{MSSEG2_dataset} is methodologically instructive, its imaging conditions and high-quality annotations make it unsuitable for real-world house inspections. Consequently, the field lacks a method for robust Siamese-based temporal comparison on small, unstructured image sets and make it equally accurate.

This thesis investigates whether an embedded Siamese approach can support pre- and post-lease change assessment in house inspections under uncontrolled visual conditions.

\textbf{Problem Statement}

Although automatic inspection tools are emerging, image-based property condition assessment is still absent in housing management. Reliance on physical inspections and tenant reports provides infrequent, inconsistent data, limiting efficient, evidence-based decision-making for housing corporations such as De Goede Woning 

\textbf{Key Research Question}

\emph{To what extent can a Siamese model with foundation-model embeddings detect and classify inspection-relevant changes between pre and post lease housing images under real world variability, and how could this use case extend to other low-data change-detection domains?}

\textbf{Sub-Research Questions}
\begin{itemize}
    \item How does a Siamese embedding-based change detection approach perform compared to baseline image similarity methods when applied to lease property images? 
    \item How can differences between Siamese image embeddings be used to distinguish and classify types of property changes, including damage, alterations, wear and tear, and maintenance needs? 
    \item To what extent is the Siamese approach robust to variations in lighting, camera angle, surface appearance, and image quality present in provided images? 
    \item How can the outputs of change detection models be translated into inspection oriented indicators that support housing staff? 

\end{itemize}
