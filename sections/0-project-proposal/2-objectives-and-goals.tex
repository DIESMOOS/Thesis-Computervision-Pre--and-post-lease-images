\section{Objectives \& Goals}
\label{sec:objectives_and_goals}

The primary objective of this project is to develop and evaluate a comparative inspection pipeline for lease housing images using two fundamentally different model paradigms.

Literature shows strong performance of supervised object-detection networks in structured inspection tasks. However, foundation models represent a different paradigm: they are trained on large scale heterogeneous data and may generalize without domain-specific supervision. While promising, their performance in structured inspection contexts is not obvious, particularly where precise category definitions and visual localization are required. This study aims to provide evidence regarding their comparative suitability.

\textbf{Datasets} \\
\begin{itemize}
\item Inhouse dataset 2000 pictures over 3 years
\item Roboflow datasets \cite{Roboflow}
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Methodological Approach} \\
\begin{itemize}
\item Data Annotation
Lease housing images will be manually labeled according to predefined inspection-relevant categories (damage, unauthorized alterations, excessive wear, maintenance-related issues). This establishes ground truth.

\item YOLO-Based Detection
A YOLO architecture will be trained on the labeled dataset to detect and localize inspection-relevant findings.

\item  Foundation Model Evaluation
ChatGPT Pro will be prompted with the same image groups to generate structured inspection outputs, including category predictions and visual indications of detected findings.

\item  Baseline Comparison
A simple baseline method (e.g., rule-based or similarity-based classification) will be implemented for reference.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Evaluation}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Metrics: Precision, recall, F1-score, and error rates will be computed against the manually labeled ground truth.

    \item Robustness Testing: Performance will be analyzed under varying image conditions (lighting, angle, clutter).
\end{itemize}
